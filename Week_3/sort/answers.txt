sort1 uses: Bubble sort

How do you know?: When the txt files are sorted order it takes shorter time as compared to the randomized and reversed txt files showing the worst case O(n^2)
and O(n) in its best case.

sort2 uses: Merge sort

How do you know?: The running times of this sort is the smallest as compared to sort1 and sort2 and there is minimal change in running time regardless of the
order of the txt files.

sort3 uses: Selection sort

How do you know?: Running time of this sort is considerably the same in sorted, random and reversed txt files showing the complexity O(n^2) in best and worst cases.
